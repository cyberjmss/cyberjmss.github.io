This chart shows the number of median hours worked by population and sex. In this graph, it is shown that females tend to work more in the lower hour ranges (< 40) while males tend to work longer, more hours. In short, females tend to work part time, while men work full time jobs with occasional overtime. The hour range where the gap is most bridged is the 35-39 hour range. The disparity can be explained by legacy gender beliefs that women stay home to do the housework, while the men head out for the work, or simply called “breadwinners”. However, due to recent advancements in society, women are starting to work more, with their modal hourly range within 0 to 40. Women could be working part-time jobs and full-time jobs. This could be due to gender roles that are commonly associated with this, namely where the wife takes care of the kids and house chores, leaving less time for work. This data shows how social and cultural norms may cause women to work less relative to men their age, hampering their potential income and career development. It also highlights the importance of challenging these social norms to ensure equality for all.
