These three charts show the ratio of employment between males and females trend closer to 1:1 from 1971 through to 2021. This is a vital statistic that highlights the potential importance of gender discrimination and stereotypical ideas such as the “housekeeping wife” throughout history, and how through time this idea has been broken down into a more fair and equal work environment. Although it's clear that vast progress has been made, we still see that from the ages of 25 - >75 males on average are more employed. This data proves that gender has historically been a key factor in one's chances of being employed, and highlights the need to continue fighting to close the gender gap once and for all.
